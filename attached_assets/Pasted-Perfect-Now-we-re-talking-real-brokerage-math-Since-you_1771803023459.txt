Instead of:

Client Price = Sub Pay + Your Margin + Risk Buffer

STEP 3 — Build It Backwards (Subcontractor Payout Formula)

If you want 30% margin:

Client Price = Sub Pay / (1 - 0.30)
Client Price = Sub Pay / 0.70

So if sub needs $210:

Client sees:

210 / 0.70 = $300

Your gross = $90 (30%)

🚀 FINAL SMART ALGORITHM (Brokerage Version)

Variables:
	•	S = sqft
	•	B = bedrooms
	•	Ba = bathrooms
	•	Basement = true/false
	•	service_type
	•	target_margin = 0.30

⸻

1️⃣ Calculate Internal Subcontractor Cost

You can negotiate subs at:

Service	Sub Base Rate
Standard	$0.10
Deep	$0.14
Move-out	$0.18

Then add complexity:

sub_base = S * sub_rate
sub_bed_adj = max(0, B - 2) * 8
sub_bath_adj = max(0, Ba - 1) * 20
sub_basement_adj = Basement ? max(40, 0.02 * S) : 0

sub_total = sub_base + sub_bed_adj + sub_bath_adj + sub_basement_adj


⸻

2️⃣ Convert To Client Price (Margin Protected)

client_price = sub_total / (1 - target_margin)
client_price = round(client_price / 5) * 5


⸻

Example

2,000 sqft | 3 bed | 2 bath | basement | standard

Sub Cost:

sub_base = 2000 * 0.10 = 200
bed_adj = 8

basement = max(40, 40) = 40

sub_total = 268

Client Price:

268 / 0.70 = 382.85 → $385

Your margin:

$117

That’s strong and still market competitive in NJ.
